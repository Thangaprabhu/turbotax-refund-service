# Held or Stopped Federal Refunds

A federal refund may be held or stopped when the IRS needs more time to process the return, verify identity, validate credits, review potential fraud, resolve offsets, or correct return issues.

## User guidance

- Show the last known refund status and the date it was last updated.
- Explain that a held or stopped refund does not always mean the user did something wrong.
- If the IRS sent correspondence, the user should respond by the deadline.
- If the refund is delayed beyond normal timeframes, the user may need to contact IRS support or Taxpayer Advocate Service depending on severity and eligibility.

## Assistant guardrail

Do not present the assistant as a tax professional or legal representative. Provide next steps and encourage official support where needed.
